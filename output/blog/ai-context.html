Context is King

Why AI Sometimes Forgets What You Just Said

Published: June 3, 2025 8 min read

Picture this: You're having a great conversation with a friend at a coffee shop. You're deep into discussing your weekend plans when suddenly, mid-sentence, they look at you with blank eyes and ask, "I'm sorry, who are you again?"

Weird, right? Well, welcome to one of AI's most human-like quirks – except instead of having a medical condition, your AI friend has something called a "context window," and understanding it is the difference between AI frustration and AI magic.

The Goldfish Memory Myth (Except It's Real for AI)

Remember that myth about goldfish having three-second memories? Turns out that's not true for goldfish, but it's surprisingly accurate for how many people think AI memory works. The reality is both more interesting and more useful once you understand it.

AI doesn't forget like humans do. It doesn't wake up one day and suddenly blank on your name. Instead, it has a very specific type of memory limitation that's more like... well, imagine trying to have a conversation while looking through a window that can only show you the last few pages of text.

Every AI model has what's called a "context window" – think of it as the amount of conversation it can "see" at any one time. Some can see the equivalent of a few pages, others can see the equivalent of a short book. But eventually, if your conversation gets long enough, the oldest parts start falling out of view.

Why This Happens (And Why It's Not Actually a Bug)

Here's the thing that blew my mind when I first learned it: this limitation isn't a flaw that developers forgot to fix. It's more like a design choice with some pretty good reasons behind it.

Speed and Cost

Imagine if every time you asked someone a question, they had to re-read every conversation you'd ever had with them before answering. That person would take forever to respond and probably charge you by the hour. AI works similarly – the more context it has to process, the slower and more expensive each response becomes.

Focus

Sometimes limitations actually help. When AI can only see the recent conversation, it stays focused on what you're currently working on instead of getting distracted by something you mentioned three hours ago.

Fresh Starts

Ever had a conversation that went down a rabbit hole and you wished you could just start over? With AI, you naturally get fresh starts as old context drops away.

The Magic Numbers You Should Know

Different AI models have different context window sizes, and understanding these numbers helps you work with them better:

ChatGPT-3.5: Can handle about 4,000 words of conversation (like a short article)
ChatGPT-4: Can handle about 8,000-32,000 words depending on the version (like a short story to a novella)
Claude: Can handle up to 100,000+ words (like a full-length book)
Some newer models: Can handle even more

But here's the practical translation: if you're having a quick back-and-forth conversation, you'll probably never hit these limits. If you're working on a long project or trying to analyze a large document, you might.

Signs Your AI is Hitting Its Memory Limit

You don't need to count words to know when you're approaching the edge. Watch for these signs:

The "What Were We Talking About?" Moment

AI suddenly seems confused about earlier parts of your conversation or asks you to remind it of something you just discussed.

The Generic Response

Instead of building on your specific conversation, AI starts giving more general, textbook-style answers.

The Contradiction

AI says something that directly contradicts what it said earlier in the conversation.

The Fresh Start

AI responds as if you're starting a brand new conversation, even though you've been chatting for a while.

Working WITH Memory Limits (Not Against Them)

Once you understand context windows, you can use them to your advantage:

Break Big Projects into Chunks

Instead of trying to discuss a 50-page document in one conversation, tackle it section by section. AI will actually give you better, more focused feedback this way.

Summarize as You Go

Every so often, ask AI to summarize what you've covered. This refreshes the key points in its active memory and helps you both stay on track.

Start Fresh When Stuck

If a conversation feels like it's going in circles or AI seems confused, sometimes the best solution is to start a new chat with a clear, specific request.

Use the "Context Reset" Trick

If you need to change topics completely, explicitly tell AI you're shifting focus. Something like "Let's switch gears completely – I want to talk about something totally different now."

The Art of Conversation Maintenance

Think of managing AI conversations like tending a garden. A little maintenance keeps everything healthy:

Every 10-15 exchanges: Check if AI still remembers the key details of what you're working on.
Every major topic shift: Give AI a quick reminder of the new context you're entering.
When things get long: Ask AI to summarize the key points so far, then continue building from there.
When you return later: If you're coming back to a conversation after a break, give AI a quick reminder of where you left off.

The Secret Superpower This Creates

Here's what's amazing: once you understand context windows, you actually become better at communicating with humans too. You start thinking more clearly about:

  • What information is actually relevant to the current discussion
  • How to give people the right amount of context without overwhelming them
  • When to start fresh vs. when to build on previous conversations
  • How to structure complex projects into manageable pieces

Making Peace with AI Memory

The key mindset shift is this: instead of seeing AI's memory limitations as a problem to solve, see them as a feature to work with. Some of my most productive AI sessions happen when I embrace the fresh start that comes with a new conversation.

It's like having a brilliant colleague who's amazing at focused, intensive work sessions but needs clear briefings when you start each new project together. Once you adjust your expectations and workflow accordingly, this colleague becomes incredibly valuable.

Your New Memory Strategy

Going forward, try this approach:

  1. Start each session with clear context – Give AI the background it needs to be helpful right away
  2. Stay aware of conversation length – Long isn't always better
  3. Summarize before switching topics – Help AI (and yourself) stay organized
  4. Embrace fresh starts – Sometimes a new conversation is exactly what you need

Remember, you're not working around a limitation – you're partnering with a different type of intelligence that happens to have a very specific way of managing information. Once you understand the rules of the game, you can play it exceptionally well.
